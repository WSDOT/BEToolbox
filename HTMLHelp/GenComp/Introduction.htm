GenComp

GenComp is a program calculates geometric section properties of a general composite section. A basic section and a composite piece are described with coordinates. The modular ratio between the two materials is defined. The position of neutral axis, area, and moment of inertia are computed. Typically, the basic section will represent a girder and the composite piece will represent a slab. Another type of section that can be modeled is a beam that is built in two stages such as a dropped cross beam.

Input

Units

Select US or SI Units

Modular Ratio

Enter the modular ratio of the two materials. n = Ecomposite/Ebasic

Basic Section

Enter the coordinates of the basic section. Coordinates can be in any direction (clockwise or counter-clockwise). It is assumed that the last point entered connects to the first point entered.

Composite Piece

Enter the coordinates of the composite piece. Coordinates can be in any direction (clockwise or counter-clockwise). It is assumed that the last point entered connects to the first point entered.

 

Use the + and - buttons to add and remove coordinate points. To remove one or more coordinates, select rows in the input grid by clicking on the left hand column and the pressing the - button.

 

Press the Update button at any time to update the output results.

 

 

 

 

 

Output

The output consists of the section properties of the basic and composite sections and a plot of the composite section.
