1 Prismatic Girder {#pgstable_prismatic_girder_view}
2 =====================
3 A prismatic girder has constant section properties.
4 
5 ## Girder Properties
6 Define the girder section properties. Use the drop down list to select a girder or select "Defined girder properties" to enter the girder properties.
7 
8 Item | Description
9 -----|------------------
10 Height | Overall height
11 Wtf | Top width
12 Wbf | Bottom width
13 Area | Cross sectional area
14 Ix | Moment of inertia about the horizontal centroidal axis
15 Iy | Moment of inertia about the vertical centroidal axis
16 Yt | Location of the centroid measured down from the top of the girder
17 L | Girder length
18 
19 ## Dead Loads
20 The self weight of the girder is computed from the section properties and the unit weight with reinforcement. Additional dead loads can be modeled.
21 
22 Item | Description
23 -----|----------------
24 Unit Weight | Unit weight of concrete used to compute modulus of elasticity
25 Unit Weight with Reinforcement | Unit weight of concrete and reinforcement. Used to compute dead load.
26 
27 ### Additional Dead Loads
28 Additional dead loads are modeld as discrete point loads. These loads could represent the weight of cast in place diaphragms.
29 
30 Use the [+] and [-] buttoms to add and delete additional dead loads.
31 
32 Item | Description
33 -----|----------------
34 X | Location of the load measured from the left end of the girder
35 P | Magnitude of the load (positive values for downward/gravity loads)
36 
37 ## Wind Characteristics
38 Enter the girder drag coefficient. This coefficient is used for computing wind pressure loading per LRFD 3.8.3.1. The Florida Department of Transportation has undertaken a significant amount of research to establish the following guidance for horizontal drag:
39 * Cd = 2.2 for I-shaped girders with no deck forms in place
40 * Cd = 1.5 for U-shaped girders with no deck forms in place
41 
42 ## Prestress Arrangement
43 The prestressing may be defined with a Simplified or Detailed arrangement. A simplified arrangement consists of parameters that describe the location of the prestressing force as well as a prestress transfer length. The prestressing force is defined on the Lifting and Hauling views. In a detailed arrangement, the location and magnitude of the prestressing force is defined at discrete locations along the girder. This is accomplished in the Lifting and Hauling views.
44 
45 Use the drop down list to select the Prestressing Arrangement
46 
47 Item | Description
48 -----|----------------
49 Simplified | A simplified prestressing arrangement will be used
50 Detailed | A detailed prestressing arrangement will be used
51 
52 ### Simplifed Arrangement Parameters
53 
54 Item | Description
55 -----|----------------
56 Prestress Transfer Length | Length from the ends of the girder that the prestress force is increased from zero to its full value
57 Ys (E) | Location of the straight strands
58 Yh1 (Fo) | Location of the harped strands at the end of the girder
59 Yh2 (Fcl) | Location of the harped strands at the harp point
60 Harp Point | Location of the harped point as a fraction of the girder length. Enter a value less than or equal to 0.5
61 Yts | Location of the temporary strands
62 
63 > NOTE: E, Fo, and Fcl are dimensions found on WSDOT standard girder sheets.
