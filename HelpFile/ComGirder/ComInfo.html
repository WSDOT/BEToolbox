Com Girder Information

Curved Composite Girder Stresses

The program "ComGir" computes the bending stresses for the top and bottom flanges of a curved composite girder. The stresses are calculated from the applied dead load and live load moments.
 

Input

Description:

a) Girder Number: The girder number. Can be a maximum of 4 numeric characters.
b) Radius to CL of Girder: May not = 0. Max: R=9*10

c) Number of Section Points: The total number of section points used in the calculation

Section Modulus

Numbers along tops of text boxes refer to the current section point you are working on. If you are working on section point #1, then you place the data within the text boxes below the "1".

Applied Moments

Max LL+I: This is moment of Largest Positive Value or Least Negative Value.
 

Output

Input data listed is the section modulus, dead load moment, curb dead load moment, and live load moment - maximum in the first case and minimum in the second case

The bending stresses in the top and bottom flanges are listed for both cases for maximum and minimum live-load moments.
