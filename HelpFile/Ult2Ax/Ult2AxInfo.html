Ult2Ax Info

Ultimate Strength Design For Biaxial
Bending And Axial Load Analysis

Ult2Ax computes the ultimate moment capacity about two perpendicular axes for a given concrete section subjected to a given axial load.  The axial load may be compression, tension, or zero.  The concrete section may contain voids and/or be composed of more than one concrete strength.  The reinforcement may be steel bars and/or 270k strands.

Input

A section may be defined by giving the section vertices relative to an arbitrary co-ordinate system.  The vertices are consecutively numbered clockwise for a section, counterclockwise for a void.
The X axis is positive to the right and the Y axis is positive upward.  All input data is positive with the following two exceptions:

1) Coordinates may be negative.
2) Axial loads: Compressive axial loads are positive.  Tensile axial loads are negative.

Description:

Concrete Coordinates

a) Number of concrete areas.  Maximum of 15.
b) Number of vertices, concrete strength in ksi.  Maximum of 50 for each area.
c) "X" and "Y" coordinates of vertex "n" in inches.  "n"=number of vertex.  Repeat for each vertex.
 

Prestress

d) Number of 270 kips strands and/or tendons.  Maximum of 50.
e) "X" and "Y" coordinates in inches, area in inches2, and prestress level of strand 'n' in ksi.
 

Individual Bars

f) Number of individuual rebars.
g) "X" and "Y" coordinates in inches and area of rebar 'n' in inches2.  'n'= rebar number.  Repeat for each rebar.
 

Lines of Rebar

h) Number of rebar lines.  Maximum of 50.
i) Area in inches2, coordinates of first bar in inches, number of bars in line, and coordinates of last bar in inches.  Repeat for each rebar line.
 

Rebar Properties

j) Rebar yield stress reduction PHI factor in ksi.
 

Computations

k) Number of readings.
l) Total degrees of rotation of the neutral axis.
m) Printing input of data ("Yes" or "No").
 

Axial Loads

n) Number of axial loads coordinates of centroid.
o) P in kips.  Repeat for each magnitude of P.

Note: P is located at the centroid of the section.  Rebar input may be as individual bars, rows of bars, or a combination.  Total number of rebars given individually, and in a line should not exceed 200.  If a void area is included in a larger concrete area and then subtracted from the section by listing coordinates counterclickwise then the void concrete strength should be indicated the same as the larger surrounding area.
 

Output:

The input data is printed out first, if desired, and is self-explanatory.  The interaction data for the section is printed out in tabular form.  This table represents the intersection of a horizontal plan on the three-dimensional interaction diagram.  The horizontal plane is the level of constant axial load.  The intersection is the Mx, My interaction curve.

The output values od Pult, Mxult, and Myult ontain the capacity reduction factor PHI.  They are computed by the program as follows:

1) The input P (frame analysis) is divided by PHI to obtain a magnified axial load.
2) Using this magnified axial load corresponding ultimate moment capacities are computed.
3) The values of axial load and moments in (2.) are then multiplied by PHI and printed out.  The results in (3.) represent the capacity of the member reduced by PHI.

The internal program computations include the strain produced by the 270k strand axial formces and as such the input external axial force does not have to be modified to include axial forces due to prestressing in the 270k strands.

Output units are kips, feet, and degrees.
