QConLE

LRFD Continuous Superstructure Analysis

QConLE computes the response of continuous bridge superstructures that can be modeled using the AASHTO simplified method. This program computes responses for dead loads, HL-93 live loads, and selected LRFD limit states along a continuous or simply-supported superstructure. Stiffness properties, distribution factors, and dead loads are entered on a per-span basis.

Instructions

  1. Use the Input frame for inputting your data. Data can be saved to a file and loaded for later use.

  2. Select your model type and analysis options and fill in all data for every span.
  3. Click on the Output frame to view analysis results .

Notes

  1. Points of interest are placed at tenth points along each span.

  2. Live loads are applied per LRFD 3.6.
  3. Single span structures will always be analyzed using simple support conditions.
  4. Span lengths, stiffnesses, and distribution factors must be positive values.
  5. For two stage models, stiffnesses must increase or stay equal from Stage I to Stage II.
  6. Loads are applied uniformly across the entire length of the superstructure
  7. Dual Tandem Train is not available for single span or simply-supported structures.
  8. Positive loads are applied downward
  9. Positive moment live load distribution factors are used when computing live load deflections
  10. Shear live load distribution factors are used when computing live load reactions
  11. The "Envelope simple and continuous analyses" option is only available for "Two Stage Simple/Continuous Spans" configurations with at least two spans.
  12. Live load reactions are computed for a full lane (i.e., all distribution factors are equal to 1.0)
  13. The optional "Low Boy" truck is defined in the commentary in LRFD 3.6.1.3.1.
  14. The Fatigue live load is applied per LRFD 3.6.1.4.

Envelope Simple and Continuous Behavior

The input option to "Envelope Simple and Continuous Behavior" allows you to run two two-Stage models simultaneously and compute the envelope of the results for these two models. The first model is a two-stage model with simple support conditions (span hinges at all piers) at all supports for both stages. The second model has simple support conditions for the first stage, but is made continuous over internal piers in the second stage. This option is only available for two-stage, multi-span, simple/continuous structure types.

This option is used in QConLE to support WSDOT simple/continuous envelope design policy for precast girder structures.

Layout of Negative Moment Distribution Factors

Examination of Section 4.6.2.2 of the LRFD Specifications shows that the span length used to compute distribution factors for negative moment is computed differently depending on the location in the span where the results are to be computed. This span length changes depending on whether a location is near an interior support (e.g., between points of contraflexure), or in a location outside of this range (in span). Although QCon LE does not compute distribution factors, it must provide the capability to support this specification requirement. Therefore, the program has input for negative moment distribution factors for both of these regions.

In general, support distribution factors are used between points of contraflexure, unless the span in question has no point of contraflexure - then the mid-span is used.
